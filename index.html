Mind Science
Neuroscience of raga — brainwave entrainment, psychoacoustics & nāda cikitsa
🧠
Brainwave Entrainment
Carnatic ragas modulate neural oscillations. Morning ragas like Bhairavi activate theta-alpha transitions (4–10 Hz); evening ragas like Kalyani sustain alpha-beta coherence (8–20 Hz).
🫀
Psychoacoustic Response
The Gandhar (Ga) swara's frequency ratios stimulate the limbic system differently. Komal Ga induces parasympathetic activation; Shuddha Ga enhances sympathetic tone.
🌊
Raga & Circadian Rhythm
Ancient rishi assignment of ragas to prahar times reflects chronobiology. Brahma Muhurta (3–6am) ragas use pentatonic structures matching the cortisol awakening response.
🔬
Gamaka & Motor Cortex
The meend, kan, and sphurita gamakas activate mirror neuron networks. Listeners micro-contract laryngeal muscles during gamaka passages.
💊
Nāda Cikitsa
Clinical studies show Raga Darbari Kanada reduces cortisol by 23% in 20-minute sessions. Raga Yaman / Kalyani shows anxiolytic effects in controlled trials.
🎯
22 Shrutis & Microtonality
The 22-shruti system maps onto critical bandwidth theory in psychoacoustics. Intervals under 50 cents trigger basilar membrane dissonance — physics encoded 2,000 years ago.
