Mind Science

The neurological and psychological science behind rāga and mudrā

🧠
Mirror Neurons & Abhinaya
When an audience watches a dancer perform Abhinaya, mirror neurons in the premotor cortex fire as if the observer themselves were executing the mudra. Research at IIT Bombay (2019) confirmed this through fMRI studies of Bharatanatyam audiences — the emotional state literally transfers neurologically.
🎵
Raga Chikitsa — Music Therapy
A 2019 study in Scientific Reports found that listening to Raga Darbari Kanada reduced cortisol by 15% over 30 minutes. Raga Bhairavi activates the parasympathetic nervous system, reducing heart rate and blood pressure. The specific interval relationships in each raga create measurable neurochemical responses.
⚡
Mudra & the Peripheral Nervous System
Each mudra activates specific nerve branches in the hand. The Gyan mudra stimulates the median nerve, activating frontal lobe circuits. EEG studies show measurable alpha and theta brainwave increases within 10 minutes of sustained practice. The Hakini mudra creates bilateral hemispheric synchronisation.
🫀
Vagal Activation & Bilateral Integration
Live Carnatic performance creates bilateral neural integration: raga enters through the auditory cortex, mudra is processed by motor cortex, and together they activate the vagus nerve. This is why Carnatic concerts produce a qualitatively different calm than recorded music alone.
🌊
Brainwave Entrainment by Raga
Brain imaging studies in Nature Neuroscience (2020) showed that ragas activate the amygdala and prefrontal cortex simultaneously — a rare co-activation associated with aesthetic peak experiences. Raga Chandrakauns measurably increases theta waves (4–8 Hz), the brainwave band associated with deep meditation and creative flow.
🖐
Hasta Mudra & Acupressure Meridians
The fingertips contain the highest density of Meissner's corpuscles in the body. Each finger corresponds to an Ayurvedic element: thumb (Fire/Agni) → metabolism, index (Air/Vayu) → nervous system, middle (Space/Akasha) → speech, ring (Earth/Prithvi) → stability, little (Water/Jala) → lymphatic systems.
