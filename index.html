22 Shrutis — microtonal positions

Indian classical music divides the octave into 22 shrutis (microtonal intervals), far finer than the 12-tone Western system. Sa and Pa are fixed (achala) at shrutis 1 and 13. The remaining 10 swaras share 20 shruti positions in pairs — Ri₂ and Ga₁ share shruti 5, Ri₃ and Ga₂ share shruti 7. This dual-occupancy gives Carnatic theory its flexibility: a single frequency can carry two names, and the emotional character shifts entirely based on that naming.

Mind Science

The neurology and psychology of rāga and mudrā

🧠
Mirror Neurons & Abhinaya
When audiences watch Abhinaya, mirror neurons in the premotor cortex fire as if the observer were executing the mudra. IIT Bombay fMRI studies (2019) confirmed emotional states transfer neurologically between performer and audience. The mudra is not symbolic — it is a direct neurological transmission.
🎵
Raga Chikitsa — Music Therapy
A 2019 Scientific Reports study found Raga Darbari Kanada reduced cortisol by 15% over 30 minutes. Raga Bhairavi activates the parasympathetic nervous system, measurably reducing heart rate and blood pressure. This is what the 2,000-year-old system of Raga Chikitsa encoded intuitively.
⚡
Mudra & the Peripheral Nervous System
Each mudra activates specific nerve branches. Gyan mudra (index-to-thumb) stimulates the median nerve and frontal lobe. EEG studies show measurable alpha and theta brainwave increases within 10 minutes. Hakini mudra (all fingertips touching) creates bilateral hemispheric synchronisation via corpus callosum.
🫀
Vagal Activation & Bilateral Integration
Live Carnatic performance creates bilateral neural integration: the raga enters through the auditory cortex, the mudra through the motor and premotor cortex, together activating the vagus nerve — the primary parasympathetic pathway. This is why live performance produces a qualitatively different calm than recorded music.
🌊
Brainwave Entrainment by Raga
Nature Neuroscience (2020) showed ragas simultaneously activate the amygdala and prefrontal cortex — a rare co-activation associated with aesthetic peak experiences. Raga Chandrakauns measurably increases theta waves (4–8 Hz). The Shankarabharanam scale activates identical neural circuits to the Western major scale — confirming universal emotional encoding in music.
🖐
Hasta Mudra & Ayurvedic Elements
Fingertips contain the highest density of Meissner's corpuscles (touch receptors) in the body. Each finger corresponds to an Ayurvedic element: thumb (Fire/Agni) → metabolism, index (Air/Vayu) → nervous system, middle (Space/Akasha) → speech, ring (Earth/Prithvi) → stability, little (Water/Jala) → lymphatic systems.

Theoretical Guide

Audava — 5-note (pentatonic): most intense, concentrated rasa.

Shadava — 6-note (hexatonic): balanced emotional texture.

Sampurna — 7-note (heptatonic): complete octave, full range.

Vakra — a zigzag scale that skips and returns is called Vakra (crooked). Common in Janya ragas.

Vaadi must be the most emphasized note in performance. Conventionally, Vaadi and Samvaadi are a 4th or 5th apart.

Sa & Pa are fixed (achala) — all ragas include Sa. Pa may be omitted in some ragas.
