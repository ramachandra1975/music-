Mind Science

The neurology and psychology of rāga and mudrā

🧠
Mirror Neurons & Abhinaya
When audiences watch Abhinaya, mirror neurons in the premotor cortex fire as if the observer were executing the mudra. IIT Bombay fMRI studies (2019) confirmed emotional states transfer neurologically between performer and audience.
🎵
Raga Chikitsa — Music Therapy
A 2019 Scientific Reports study found Raga Darbari Kanada reduced cortisol by 15% over 30 minutes. Bhairavi activates the parasympathetic nervous system, reducing heart rate and blood pressure. Specific interval relationships create measurable neurochemical responses.
⚡
Mudra & Peripheral Nervous System
Each mudra activates specific nerve branches. Gyan mudra stimulates the median nerve and frontal lobe. EEG studies show measurable alpha and theta brainwave increases within 10 minutes. Hakini mudra creates bilateral hemispheric synchronisation.
🫀
Vagal Activation & Bilateral Integration
Live Carnatic performance creates bilateral neural integration: raga through the auditory cortex, mudra through motor cortex, together activating the vagus nerve. This explains why concerts produce a qualitatively different calm than recorded music alone.
🌊
Brainwave Entrainment by Raga
Nature Neuroscience (2020) showed ragas simultaneously activate the amygdala and prefrontal cortex — a rare co-activation associated with aesthetic peak experiences. Raga Chandrakauns measurably increases theta waves (4–8 Hz), associated with deep meditation.
🖐
Hasta Mudra & Ayurvedic Elements
Fingertips contain the highest density of Meissner's corpuscles. Each finger corresponds to an element: thumb (Fire/Agni) → metabolism, index (Air/Vayu) → nervous system, middle (Space/Akasha) → speech, ring (Earth/Prithvi) → stability, little (Water/Jala) → lymphatic.
